PPE Risk Calculator: Mask Performance

The PPE Risk Calculators are a collection of modules to facilitate the use of statistically-based and computationally-based models to estimate PPE performance. These models fall under the category of Regulatory Science Tools (RSTs). Please click on the information icon for more details about each category.

RST qualified modules

• Mask Performance Risk Calculator

A tool to conduct screening level risk assessment of surgical respirators and surgical masks to aid in the performance evaluation of textile materials used in the construction of surgical respirators and surgical masks. These assessments can assist PPE manufacturers by providing instantaneous feedback on whether their textile materials would require additional justification and/or testing to demonstrate acceptable performance.

• Gown Performance Risk Calculator

This module can be used similarly to the mask performance risk calculator, but this module uses data measurements from textile materials used in gowns as well as providing a different performance metric since gown performance is based on liquid and/or bacterial penetration.

Medical Device Development Tools (MDDTs)

The modules in this category have been qualified as Medical Device Development Tools (MDDTs). This qualification means that the output of the tool will be accepted by the FDA without the need to reconfirm the suitability and utility of the tool, as long as it is used with the qualified context of use. For more information about MDDTs please follow the link here.

Regulatory Science Tools (RSTs)

The modules in this category have been qualified as Regulatory Science Tools (RSTs). RSTs are tools developed by the FDA intended to assist in the design and development of medical devices and test methodologies. RSTs can be used to support regulatory submissions, but suitability is assessed on a case-by-case basis. These tools may become MDDTs in the future. For more information about RSTs please follow the link here.

Development modules
